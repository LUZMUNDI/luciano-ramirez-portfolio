Jeet Kune Do München

Private martial arts studio, Munich — brand identity, website & content campaign

Objective

Increase local awareness and generate trial class enquiries for a private Munich studio where Sifu Dominik has taught Jeet Kune Do, Wing Chun, self-defence, and Chen Tai Chi since 1992. The challenge: position a deeply personal, philosophy-driven school in a market dominated by fitness-oriented martial arts clubs.

Target Audience

Men aged 20–45 in Munich. Not gym-goers — seekers. Men disillusioned with superficial training, drawn to discipline, structure, and real-world self-defence. They value authenticity and are put off by aggressive fitness marketing. Training happens in an exclusive group where everyone receives individual attention — it feels like an invitation, not a membership.

Market Gap

Most Munich martial arts schools present themselves as either fitness-oriented or hobby-based. Neither speaks to someone who wants serious, transformative, practical training. JKDM's positioning: raw, focused, no fitness-marketing clichés. A school that doesn't need to convince — it simply shows itself.

Brand Identity

Built a complete brand system from scratch: a 17-page guidelines document covering tone of voice, a 5-colour palette drawn from the studio's materials — ink, rice paper, brass, stone — typography hierarchy using Cormorant Garamond and Inter, logo rules, photography direction, and web layout principles. Every decision has a reason.

Content Strategy

Designed a 3-format video system, each with a distinct role: a 60s hero video (Sifu talking head + B-roll) to establish narrative and credibility; a 20s short-form cut optimised for Reels/TikTok as a conversion trigger; and a 13s POV video of a student executing 4 techniques to create immersion and reduce the perceived distance to training.

Make America Play Again

Evil Villains Battle Pack™ — fictional product launch campaign

The Insight

Every celebrity launches something — tequila, candles, makeup. I imagined what President Trump would launch, something he hasn't come up with yet. Then it hit me: an action figure of himself, fighting real-world villains. Pop culture has always turned its biggest figures into collectibles. So this campaign takes that logic and runs with it: geopolitics as a collector's toy universe. The concept proved culturally timely — in February 2026, RFK Jr. shared a viral video of Trump as an action figure that racked up 14K likes and thousands of shares. The appetite was already there.

Target Audience

Adult collectors who grew up on 2000s action figures — not children. People who understand the cultural reference, appreciate the satire, and would genuinely display this on a shelf. The campaign also targets viral-native audiences on TikTok who engage with politically charged pop culture content.

Creative Concept

The Evil Villains Battle Pack™ — 3 highly detailed figures, limited to 500 units at $2,000. Hero: Trump. Villains: Maduro & Khamenei. Retro 2000s packaging with explosive graphics, comic book fonts, and a collector display window. The toy commercial the 2000s never had.

Multi-Channel Strategy

Three activation channels: TikTok Toy Battles — short viral videos of kids recreating exaggerated battle scenes (#BattleTheVillains); Retro Toy Store Takeover — dramatic shelf displays with Make America Play Again branding; ComicCon Activation — giant figures, pose stations, instant social upload.

Key Learning

The strongest creative ideas come from a single sharp cultural observation. The entire campaign — video, site, packaging, channels — flows from one insight: there's a hero fighting real villains, and the world has always turned that into a toy. Everything else is execution.

Jesus Haus München

Outreach campaign, content creation & community building — Munich church network

The Objective

Jesus Haus München runs free German language classes — Kostenlose Deutschkurse — aimed at Munich's expat and migrant communities. The goal: increase attendance significantly and build an active online community that kept people engaged between sessions.

Outreach Strategy

Took the campaign offline — visiting dozens of refugee camps across Munich to connect directly with the target audience. Documented the journey in a photo carousel shared on social media, turning outreach into content. Simultaneously built an online community on Meetup for people to sign up and stay connected.

Result

Attendance at the Kostenlose Deutschkurse doubled. The outreach approach — combining physical presence at refugee camps with digital community building — proved that meeting people where they are, both online and offline, is the most effective strategy for this audience.

Content Created

Produced social content for the Jesus Haus München Instagram — including a 7-slide outreach documentary carousel, topical content reacting to current events (edited split-screen video of the pastor responding to the Iran situation), and ongoing community posts to keep the audience active and engaged.

What's Next

I identified an opportunity: the church network is rebranding under a unified identity — and has no website proposal yet. I am self-initiating a full brand proposal — identity system, brand guidelines, and website — to pitch to the network. Active project, in development.

JKDM Brand Guidelines

Complete brand identity system — 16 pages — Jeet Kune Do München

What It Is

A complete, self-contained brand identity document for Jeet Kune Do München — a private martial arts studio in Munich where Sifu Dominik has taught since 1992. Built from scratch with no brief except the school itself: its philosophy, its teacher, its audience.

The Strategic Foundation

Positioned against every generic martial arts school — no aggression, no fitness clichés, no warrior mythology. The brand is built around three principles: concrete over abstract, less is more, and short and direct. A school that doesn't need to convince — it simply shows itself.

The Visual System

Five colours — each sourced from the studio's own material vocabulary: ink (Sumi), rice paper (Reis), brass (Messing), stone (Stein), and ink-stone red (Tusche). Two typefaces: Cormorant Garamond for display, Inter for body. Four Chinese characters with fixed semantic roles. Every decision has a reason.

Scope

10 sections across 16 pages: Introduction & Tone of Voice · Logo Rules · Colour System · Typography · Imagery & Chinese Characters · Web Layout System · Language Examples · Vocabulary · Content Sections · Technical Recommendations · Pre-launch Checklist.

Why It Matters

Brand consistency is not a design problem — it's a strategy problem. These guidelines give anyone who works with JKDM a shared language for every creative decision, from a social post to a full website rebuild. Built to last, not just for the current site.
