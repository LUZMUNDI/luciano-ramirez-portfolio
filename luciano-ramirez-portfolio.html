Web Brand Strategy

Jeet Kune Do Munich

Local martial arts school — full digital presence

The Brief

A local Jeet Kune Do school in Munich needed a digital presence that reflected the philosophy and discipline of the art — not just a generic sports website.

Audience Insight

Research revealed two core audiences: adults seeking personal development through martial arts, and parents looking for structured, values-based training for their children.

Strategy

Positioned the school around Bruce Lee's philosophy: adaptability, self-expression, and discipline. The site communicates depth — not just schedules and prices.

Deliverables

Website built and live · Brand guidelines · Audience research document · 3 pieces of video content

Campaign Video Web

Action Figure Campaign

Fictional product launch — end-to-end creative campaign

The Concept

A self-initiated fictional product launch to demonstrate full campaign thinking — from audience definition through to final video and website.

Creative Strategy

Built around nostalgia and collectibility — targeting adult collectors, not children. The tone, visuals, and copy all serve that positioning.

Deliverables

Promotional video · Campaign website · Audience research · Creative brief

Key Learning

Fictional briefs are real briefs — every decision still needs to be justified by audience insight and brand logic.

Web Brand Content

Local Church — Digital Presence

Community-first website, brand guidelines, and content

The Challenge

A local church needed to reach new community members digitally — without losing the warmth and authenticity that defines in-person community.

Approach

Led with people, not programs. The site centers human stories and community moments rather than event listings and service times.

Deliverables

Status
